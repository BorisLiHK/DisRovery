DisRovery

Objective

Collect samples and survive as long as you can.

Background

You have been selected to control an unmanned rover on a foreign planet to collect local mineral samples for further research.

Supplies have been sent randomly over the planet surface to assist your mission. However, it is not recommended to pick up every supplies on the way since each has its own positive and negative effects on the rover.

Destination planets each has its own characteristics and potential hazards, while collecting supplies might help, you should pick your rover wisely with the destination in mind.

Game Over

The game ends under the two conditions,

  • Power runs out; or
  • Shield runs out.

Supplies

Power Cell

Recharges rover's power with no side effects.

While you can also recharge the rover by staying stationary, try not to miss any power cells on your way since they causes no harm at all.

Engine

Increases power consumption in exchange for thrust power.

Everything has its weight, collecting samples will eventually slows down your rover. Collecting engines is the only way to compensate the drag of mass.

Transistor

Reduces power consumption and improves recharging rate, but makes rover more vulnerable.

Transistors boost the performance of the rover, extending the time you can stay moving and reducing the time being stationary needed for recharging.

Tools

Fixes rover's shield and makes rover less vulnerable, but also makes rover heavier and lowers recharge rate.

Collecting tools is the only to fix rover's shield, it also improves rover's endurance and defense meaning that you can use turbo for a longer period of time.
