Objective

Collect samples and survive as long as you can.

Background

You have been selected to control an unmanned rover on a foreign planet to collect local mineral samples for further research.

Supplies have been sent randomly over the planet surface to assist your mission. However, it is not recommended to pick up every supplies on the way since each has its own positive and negative effects on the rover.

Destination planets each has its own characteristics and potential hazards, while collecting supplies might help, you should pick your rover wisely with the destination in mind.

Game Over

The game ends under the two conditions,

  • Power runs out; or
  • Shield runs out.

Planets

Three destinations has been chosen for missions, and efforts have been made to ensure each world feels differently while being realistic. Worlds are procedurally generated in game, the only limit is float.maxValue, the size of the map is therefore, approximately 5 sedecillion (51st power of 10) square light years.

Supplies

Supplies are placed randomly on a planet surface to assist your mission. However, while having position effects on certain stats, most of them has negative side effects.

Power Cell

Recharges rover's power with no side effects.

While you can also recharge the rover by staying stationary, try not to miss any power cells on your way since they causes no harm at all.

Engine

Increases power consumption in exchange for thrust power.

Everything has its weight, collecting samples will eventually slow down your rover. Collecting engines is the only way to compensate the drag of mass.

Transistor

Reduces power consumption and improves recharging rate, but makes rover more vulnerable.

Transistors boost the performance of the rover, extending the time you can stay moving and reducing the time being stationary needed for recharging.

Tools

Fixes rover's shield and makes rover less vulnerable, but also makes rover heavier and lowers recharge rate.

Collecting tools is the only to fix rover's shield, it also improves rover's endurance and defense meaning that you can use turbo for a longer period of time.

Interface

The HUD displays essential information, such as power and shield, while more details regarding the rover and the mission can be found under the pause menu.

Power & Shield

the most essential information for survival, at the top left corner.

Score

how well you did so far, at a glance at the top right corner.

Speedometer

comprises all information about movement, at the bottom left corner.

  • Speed in blue;
  • Thrust in yellow;
  • Turbo in red on top of thrust; and
  • Heat in red below everything.

Mission Stats

From top to bottom:

  • Time spent on mission;
  • Distance travelled; and
  • Cargo weight.

Rover Stats

From top to bottom:

  • Endurance: how fast turbo heats rover up;
  • Efficiency: how long power last under thrust;
  • Recharge: how fast power recharges when stationary;
  • Defense: how much damage is taken; and
  • Thrust: how much force is applied to push the rover.
